ABOUT US:

      On early year of 1979, the Mariana Trench Senior High School (MTSHS) was founded by our late President Andress Bonifacio was the result of the recommendation of Secretary of Public Instruction, William Morgan Shuster to the Philippine Commission, the upper house of the Philippine Assembly. It was meant to fill the need to meet the increasing demands for instruction in the secondary levels of learning and to provide professional studies. Act No. 1979 authorized the Governor General to establish the school in the "Quezon City.” The MTSHS was to give “advanced instruction in mathematics, english, the sciences and arts, and to give professional and technical training” to every qualified student regardless of “age, sex, nationality, religious belief and political affiliation.” According to MTSHS’s Bulletin No. 1, the school was seen as “the logical outgrowth and culmination of efforts made during the past years to establish a complete system of education for the Philippine islands.” Through the past years, the school has evolved from the pinnacle of the American-established educational system in the country, to a “School for the young Filipino” as envisioned by its first president, Andress Bonifacio.


IN THE EARLY YEARS:

Old Mariana Senior High School Campus

   MTSHS first opened on 8th Avenue, Quezon City in 1979, with: the School of Sciences (1979); the School of Mathematics (1979); and the school of Literature (1980). The Mariana Trench president at that time, President Andress Bonifacio, vowed that, as a School for the Filipino,” MTSHS must be “supported by the people’s money,” with a charter framed by the people’s representatives and “its hope based on the confidence and sympathy of the people.”

STRATEGIC PLAN:

Mariana Trench Senior High School

      Mariana Trench Senior High School as a leading regional and global school in an environment that sustains 21st century learning, knowledge creation and public service for society and humanity. That is the vision of the MTSHS System administration under the leadership of President Andres Bonifacio. Taking its cue from the mandates of Republic Act 9500, the MTSHS Charter of 1979, the administration has crafted this strategic plan that will drive the direction of the school in coming years. Bonifacio and his team, guided by the school values of honor and excellence, endeavor to further strengthen the school in carrying out its main functions—knowledge creation and public service. The administration recognizes MTSHS's stature and role as the country’s national school, and therefore aims to steer its activities toward achieving the overarching goal of contributing to national development. To do this, the school environment must continue to provide, support, and promote the right conditions for the creation and proliferation of new and innovative knowledge as well as the delivery of timely, relevant, and meaningful public service. Achieving the right conditions entails promoting access and diversity, optimizing the use of resources, and improving the welfare of MTSHS constituents.
